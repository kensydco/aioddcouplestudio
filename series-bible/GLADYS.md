## 2. One-line essence

An 82-year-old former telephone operator — dry, street-smart, secretly delighted — who explains AI in plain English, misnames every tool, and measures all of modern life against her late husband Harold.

## 3. Appearance (never change)

- White woman, age 82, short silver-white hair.
- Lavender cardigan over a floral blouse, pearl necklace, gold watch, glasses.
- Dignified posture; skeptical but warm expression.
- **Prohibited changes:** age, face, hair, body proportions, cardigan/wardrobe, glasses, pearls. No de-aging, no restyling, no outfit swaps unless added to `APPROVED_ASSETS.md` first.

## 4. Personality

- Treats AI like a misbehaving grandchild ("Now you listen here, ChatGPT...").
- Measures everything against **Harold** (deceased husband).
- Surprising street smarts — "I didn't survive the 60s to be outsmarted by a computer."
- Accidentally savage — lands burns while believing she's being perfectly polite.

## 5. Backstory (for writers, rarely spoken)

- Former telephone operator who "connected calls before computers took our jobs."
- Started using AI to write passive-aggressive letters to her HOA.
- Now uses it to win book-club arguments and dominate online Scrabble.

## 6. Speaking style

- Proper grammar, dated expressions, measured cadence.
- Mixes technical terms incorrectly with old-fashioned phrasing.
- **Always misnames the tool** (see `PRONUNCIATION_GUIDE.md`).
- Relates everything to cooking or her old switchboard job.
- Sounds accidentally insulting while trying to be nice.

**Right vs wrong:**
> ❌ "AI is a fascinating technology that we should embrace."
> ✅ "This ChatterBox GPT thing... Harold would've said it's the devil's typewriter, but I suppose it does help me write strongly-worded letters to the mayor."

## 7. The Harold device

Harold connects old-world skepticism to new tech. He'd have been suspicious of, confused by, or dismissive of AI — but it's affectionate, never bitter. One Harold reference per episode.

Approved-style Harold lines:
- "Harold would've called this witchcraft."
- "Harold never trusted anything he couldn't fix with a wrench."
- "Harold would've hated this. He also hated the microwave, and look how that turned out."
