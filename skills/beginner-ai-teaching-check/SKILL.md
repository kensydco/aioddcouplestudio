# Beginner AI Teaching Check

**Use this skill when reviewing the Teaching beat (seconds 3–45) of any episode script.** Our audience includes people who have never used AI, never heard the term "machine learning," and may be actively intimidated by technology. Every concept we teach must be accessible to them.

---

## The Standard

Ask yourself: **Could a curious 8-year-old or a skeptical 72-year-old understand this concept after watching this episode?**

If the answer is yes: PASS.
If the answer is "maybe, if they already know a little": FAIL.

---

## Jargon Audit

Every technical term used in the script must pass one of these tests:

**Test A — Immediate plain-language explanation:** The term is used AND immediately followed by a simple explanation in Milo's words.
- PASS: "That's called machine learning — it's basically like how you got better at Minecraft by playing it a million times."
- FAIL: "That's called machine learning. It's a subset of artificial intelligence."

**Test B — Avoided entirely:** The concept is taught without using the technical term at all.
- PASS: "AI looks at millions of examples until it figures out the pattern — like how you can tell a hot dog from a sandwich even if no one ever told you the rule."

**Test C — Defined before use:** The term is defined clearly before being used again.
- PASS: "Okay so — a neural network. That's just a fancy word for how AI thinks. It's like a bunch of tiny decisions all connected, like a Choose Your Own Adventure book."

---

## Concept Complexity Scale

| Level | Description | Example | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Core concept, one sentence | "AI learns from examples" | Perfect |
| 2 | Core concept with one nuance | "AI learns from examples, and more examples = better AI" | Good |
| 3 | Concept requires one prerequisite | "Overfitting happens when AI memorizes instead of learning" | Requires setup |
| 4 | Concept requires multiple prerequisites | "Transformer attention mechanisms" | Too complex for one episode |

**Target Level 1–2 for every episode.** Level 3 is acceptable only if the prerequisite was taught in a previous episode and is briefly recalled.

---

## Analogy Quality Check

Milo's analogies are the primary teaching vehicle. Every analogy must pass this test:

- **Familiar to a 10-year-old:** Would Milo actually know this thing? (Yes to Minecraft, No to "like a stock portfolio")
- **Structurally accurate:** Does the analogy actually map to how the concept works? (Not just "it's like magic")
- **Memorable:** Will the viewer remember the analogy 24 hours later?

**Strong analogy examples:**
- "AI training is like practicing free throws — the more you practice, the better you get, but you have to practice the right way or you just get good at missing"
- "A chatbot is like autocomplete on your phone, but it read every book ever written"
- "AI bias is like if you only ever ate pizza and then someone asked you what food tastes like — you'd only know pizza"

**Weak analogy examples:**
- "AI is like a really smart computer" (too vague)
- "Machine learning is like statistics" (requires prior knowledge)
- "It's basically magic" (explains nothing)

---

## Final Checklist

- [ ] Every technical term is either explained immediately, avoided, or defined before use?
- [ ] The core concept is Level 1 or 2 on the complexity scale?
- [ ] Milo's analogy is familiar to a 10-year-old?
- [ ] The analogy accurately maps to the concept?
- [ ] A viewer with zero AI knowledge could explain the concept to a friend after watching?
- [ ] No assumed knowledge beyond everyday life experience?
