# Episode Format Enforcer

**Use this skill when writing or reviewing any episode script.** Every episode MUST follow the four-beat structure below. No exceptions. No reordering. No skipping beats.

---

## The Four-Beat Structure

### Beat 1: Cold Open (Seconds 0–3)
**Purpose:** Hook the viewer before they can scroll away.

**Requirements:**
- Milo and Gladys must already be mid-argument or mid-situation when the video starts — no introductions, no "Hi everyone"
- The conflict or question must be immediately clear
- Must create one of these reactions in the viewer: curiosity ("wait, what?"), conflict ("who's right?"), or humor ("this is already chaotic")
- The AI concept being taught should be implied but not yet named

**Pass criteria:** A stranger watching with no context should be confused in an interesting way within 3 seconds.

**Fail criteria:** Any opening that starts with a greeting, an introduction, or a calm statement.

**Example PASS:** *(Milo slams laptop shut)* "Gladys, I TOLD you — it's not cheating, it's just asking the computer to think for you!" / "That IS cheating, Milo. Harold never needed a computer to think."

**Example FAIL:** "Hi everyone, today Milo and Gladys are going to talk about AI."

---

### Beat 2: Teaching (Seconds 3–45)
**Purpose:** Deliver the educational content through character-driven dialogue.

**Requirements:**
- Teach exactly ONE AI concept — never two
- Milo must explain the concept using at least one analogy from his world
- Gladys must misunderstand or reframe the concept in a funny but logical way
- The concept must be explainable to a complete beginner (no assumed knowledge)
- Milo's explanation must be the primary teaching vehicle

**Pass criteria:** A viewer with zero AI knowledge should understand the concept by the end of this beat.

**Fail criteria:** Two concepts introduced. Jargon used without immediate plain-language explanation. Gladys correctly understands on first try.

**Concept difficulty guide:**
- Too hard: "Transformer architecture uses self-attention mechanisms"
- Just right: "AI learns by looking at millions of examples, like how you learned to recognize a dog by seeing a thousand dogs"
- Too easy: "AI is a computer" (not enough to teach)

---

### Beat 3: Demo / Chaos (Seconds 45–75)
**Purpose:** Show the concept in action through an absurd, comedic demonstration.

**Requirements:**
- Must demonstrate the AI concept practically — even if the demo goes wrong
- The comedy escalates here — this is the highest-energy beat
- Gladys's misunderstanding should peak in this beat
- The demo should be something a viewer could actually try (or imagine trying)
- Can include: Gladys accidentally using AI correctly, Milo's demo backfiring, or an unexpected result that proves the concept

**Pass criteria:** The viewer sees the concept applied in a memorable, funny way.

**Fail criteria:** No demo or practical application. Pure dialogue with no action. Comedy that requires prior knowledge to understand.

---

### Beat 4: Resolution (Seconds 75–90)
**Purpose:** Land the lesson and leave the viewer warm.

**Requirements:**
- Gladys must arrive at her own understanding of the concept — in her own words, through her own metaphor
- The episode must end with warmth — they are a team, even when they argue
- Must include EITHER Milo's catchphrase ("See? Easy.") OR a Harold callback
- The viewer should feel like they learned something AND smiled

**Pass criteria:** The episode ends with the viewer understanding the concept AND feeling good about Milo and Gladys.

**Fail criteria:** Episode ends on conflict. Gladys never "gets it." No warmth. No Harold or catchphrase.

---

## Full Episode Checklist

Run this before approving any script:

- [ ] Cold open starts mid-action, no greeting or introduction?
- [ ] Hook creates curiosity or conflict within 3 seconds?
- [ ] Exactly ONE AI concept is taught?
- [ ] Milo uses at least one kid-world analogy?
- [ ] Gladys mispronounces or misnames the technology?
- [ ] Harold is mentioned at least once?
- [ ] Demo or practical application is included?
- [ ] Episode ends with warmth and unity?
- [ ] Milo's catchphrase OR Harold callback in the resolution?
- [ ] Total estimated runtime is 60–90 seconds?
- [ ] All content passes PG filter?

---

## Timing Reference

At average speaking pace (150 words/minute), a 60–90 second episode = **150–225 words of dialogue**. Scripts should target 175–200 words for comfortable pacing with visual pauses.
