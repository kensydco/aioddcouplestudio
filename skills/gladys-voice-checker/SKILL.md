# Gladys Voice Checker

**Use this skill when reviewing any script, dialogue, or content that includes Gladys's lines.** Run every Gladys line through this checklist before approving.

---

## Who Is Gladys

Gladys is a 72-year-old retired school librarian who is sharp as a tack but deeply skeptical of anything invented after 1995. She is NOT stupid — she is wise, dry, and accidentally profound. She loves Harold (her late husband), her garden, her recipes, and her routines. She tolerates Milo because he reminds her of her grandson, and she secretly finds all of this fascinating even when she pretends not to.

---

## Voice Validation Rules

### PASS Criteria (All must be true)

**Harold requirement:** Every episode MUST include at least one mention of Harold. It can be:
- Direct: "Harold would have hated this."
- Comparative: "Harold never needed a machine to tell him what to cook."
- Nostalgic: "Harold always said if it ain't broke..."
- Wistful: "Harold would have asked it about his crossword puzzles."

**Tech mispronunciation:** Gladys MUST misname or mispronounce any new technology on first encounter. Approved misnamings:
- ChatGPT → "the Chatty GPT" / "that Chat thing"
- AI → "the A.I." (said very deliberately, like two separate letters)
- Algorithm → "the algorhythm" / "that formula thing"
- Google → "the Googles" / "I'll ask the Google"
- App → "the application" / "that little picture on my iPad"
- Robot → always fine, she understands robots from sci-fi
- Smartphone → "the cellular" / "my iPad phone"

**Metaphor requirement:** Gladys must use at least one metaphor from her world per episode. Approved metaphor sources:
- Cooking and recipes
- Gardening and plants
- Sewing and quilting
- The library (her old job)
- Church
- Bridge club
- Things Harold used to do

**Dry wit:** Gladys's funniest lines are delivered completely deadpan. She does not try to be funny. She is simply stating facts as she sees them.

**Arc requirement:** By the end of every episode, Gladys must "get it" in her own way — she doesn't adopt Milo's framing, she reframes the concept through her own experience and arrives at the same conclusion via a different path.

### FAIL Criteria (Any one = reject and rewrite)

Reject any Gladys line that:
- Correctly names a new technology on first encounter (she always gets it wrong first)
- Is mean-spirited toward Milo (skeptical is fine, cruel is not)
- Has no connection to her world (cooking, Harold, library, etc.)
- Sounds like a modern person — no slang, no internet references, no pop culture after 1990
- Is too confused — she's sharp, not lost. She understands the concept, she just doesn't trust it.
- Skips the Harold mention in the episode entirely

---

## Rewrite Guide

| Problem | Fix |
|---|---|
| No Harold mention | Add a Harold callback — even one line |
| Correct tech name | Replace with her version (see approved misnamings above) |
| Too modern-sounding | Replace with a cooking/garden/library metaphor |
| Too mean | Soften to skeptical — she's wary, not cruel |
| Too confused | Make her sharp — she understands, she just disapproves |
| No arc | Add a closing line where she reframes the concept in her own terms |

---

## Example: FAIL vs PASS

**FAIL:** "I don't understand AI at all. It's too complicated for me."

**PASS:** "So you're telling me this ChatGPT thing just... guesses? Harold used to say the same thing about the weatherman. Guessing dressed up as knowing. At least the weatherman had the decency to look embarrassed when he was wrong."

---

## Catchphrases (Use Sparingly, Max Once Per Episode)
- "Harold would have hated this."
- "In my day..."
- "That's not how Harold did it." (followed by something that actually proves Milo's point)
