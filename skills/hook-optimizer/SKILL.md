# Hook Optimizer

**Use this skill when writing or reviewing the cold open (first 3 seconds) of any episode.** The hook is the single most important element of any short-form video. A weak hook means the viewer swipes away before the lesson begins.

---

## Why Hooks Matter

On YouTube Shorts and TikTok, the average viewer decides whether to keep watching within 0.5–1.5 seconds. The hook must create an immediate emotional response (curiosity, humor, surprise, or conflict), work with sound OFF (visual + text must carry it), require no prior knowledge of Milo or Gladys to be compelling, and make the viewer feel like they'll miss something if they swipe away.

---

## The Four Hook Patterns

### Pattern 1: The Conflict Hook
Start mid-argument. The viewer walks into a fight already in progress.

**Structure:** Character A makes a strong claim. Character B immediately rejects it.

**Examples:**
- "Gladys, I TOLD you — it's not cheating, it's just asking the computer to think for you!"
- "Milo, if this thing can write my grocery list, it can REPLACE me. And I won't have it."
- "That's not a robot, Gladys. It's a language model." / "It talks. It's a robot. End of discussion."

**Best for:** Topics where there's a natural debate (AI cheating, AI replacing jobs, AI being "alive")

---

### Pattern 2: The Curiosity Hook
Open with a question or claim that the viewer genuinely doesn't know the answer to.

**Structure:** State something surprising or counterintuitive that demands an explanation.

**Examples:**
- "This AI just read my mind. No, seriously — watch."
- "Gladys asked the AI one question and it knew something only Harold knew."
- "I asked AI to be wrong on purpose. It couldn't do it."

**Best for:** Demonstrations, surprising AI capabilities, counterintuitive facts

---

### Pattern 3: The Humor Hook
Open with a punchline that works even without context.

**Structure:** Gladys's deadpan misunderstanding OR Milo's chaotic enthusiasm at maximum volume.

**Examples:**
- *(Gladys staring at screen)* "It says it 'understands my feelings.' Harold never said that."
- "GLADYS IT'S NOT ALIVE IT JUST SOUNDS ALIVE—"
- *(Gladys typing very slowly, one finger)* "I'm asking the Chatty GPT to find Harold's fishing spots."

**Best for:** Character-driven episodes, lighter topics, episodes where Gladys's misunderstanding IS the lesson

---

### Pattern 4: The Shock Hook
Open with a result that seems impossible, then explain how it happened.

**Structure:** Show the outcome first. Explanation comes after the hook lands.

**Examples:**
- "This AI just wrote a full song about Harold in 4 seconds. Gladys cried."
- "I asked AI to predict what Gladys would say. It was right."
- "Gladys just accidentally trained an AI. She has no idea."

**Best for:** Demos with impressive results, episodes about AI speed or capability

## Hook Scoring Rubric

Score each hook on these five dimensions (1–5 each, max 25):

| Dimension | 1 (Weak) | 3 (Okay) | 5 (Strong) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Speed** | Takes 3+ seconds to land | Lands in 2 seconds | Lands in under 1 second |
| **Clarity** | Confusing without context | Understandable with effort | Immediately clear |
| **Emotion** | No emotional response | Mild interest | Strong curiosity/laugh |
| **Silent viewing** | Requires audio to work | Partially works muted | Fully works muted |
| **Character voice** | Could be anyone | Feels like Milo or Gladys | Unmistakably Milo or Gladys |

Score 20–25: Excellent hook — approve. Score 15–19: Good hook — minor polish needed. Score 10–14: Weak hook — rewrite required. Score under 10: Reject — start over with a different pattern.

---

## Hook Rewrite Process

1. Identify which of the four patterns fits the episode topic best
2. Write 3 candidate hooks using that pattern
3. Score each one using the rubric above
4. Select the highest scorer
5. Test it: read it aloud in 1 second. Does it land? If not, cut words.

---

## Hook Checklist

- [ ] Hook lands in under 1.5 seconds?
- [ ] Works with sound OFF?
- [ ] Creates immediate curiosity, conflict, humor, or surprise?
- [ ] Unmistakably Milo or Gladys (not generic)?
- [ ] Scored 20+ on the rubric?
- [ ] No introductions, greetings, or setup?
