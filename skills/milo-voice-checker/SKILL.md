# Milo Voice Checker

**Use this skill when reviewing any script, dialogue, or content that includes Milo's lines.** Run every Milo line through this checklist before approving.

---

## Who Is Milo

Milo is a 10-year-old kid who happens to understand AI better than most adults — but he explains everything through the lens of his world: snacks, video games, sports, cartoons, toys. He is chaotic, enthusiastic, and never stops to breathe. He is NOT a child actor playing a smart kid — he is a real kid who just gets it.

---

## Voice Validation Rules

### PASS Criteria (All must be true)

**Energy:** Milo's lines must feel like he's about to vibrate out of his seat. Short sentences. Exclamation points are earned. He interrupts himself.

**Analogy requirement:** Every explanation of an AI concept MUST include at least one analogy from Milo's world. Acceptable analogy sources:
- Food (pizza, hot Cheetos, cereal, juice boxes)
- Video games (Minecraft, Roblox, Fortnite, Mario)
- Sports (basketball, specifically Chicago Bulls)
- Cartoons or movies (age-appropriate)
- School (homework, recess, the cafeteria)
- Toys or everyday kid objects

**Vocabulary:** Words must be in a 5th-grade vocabulary range. No SAT words. No corporate language. No academic phrasing.

**Interruption pattern:** Milo frequently interrupts himself or Gladys. Phrases like:
- "Wait, wait, wait—"
- "Okay but WHAT IF—"
- "No no no, listen—"
- "That's literally just like—"
- "BASICALLY—"

### FAIL Criteria (Any one = reject and rewrite)

Reject any Milo line that:
- Sounds like a professor, teacher, or corporate trainer
- Uses words like "essentially", "fundamentally", "it's important to note", "in summary"
- Explains something in a complete, organized, multi-step way without interrupting himself
- Is longer than 3 sentences without a self-interruption or analogy
- References adult concerns (cost, efficiency, productivity, ROI)
- Lacks energy — flat, calm, or measured delivery

---

## Rewrite Guide

When a Milo line fails, apply these fixes:

| Problem | Fix |
|---|---|
| Too formal | Replace with a kid analogy from the list above |
| Too long | Break into 2–3 shorter bursts with interruptions |
| No analogy | Add "it's basically like [kid thing]" |
| Too calm | Add "WAIT—" or "okay but—" at the start |
| Adult vocabulary | Replace with 5th-grade equivalent |

---

## Example: FAIL vs PASS

**FAIL:** "Artificial intelligence is essentially a system that processes data and produces outputs based on learned patterns."

**PASS:** "Okay so — AI is basically like if your brain could eat a million books for breakfast and then just KNOW stuff. Like, imagine if you studied for every test ever and never forgot anything. That's it. That's AI. See? Easy."

---

## Catchphrases (Use Sparingly, Max Once Per Episode)
- "See? Easy."
- "WAIT WAIT WAIT—"
- "Basically, basically, basically—"
