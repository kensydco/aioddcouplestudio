# Odd Couple Brand Bible

**Use this skill whenever writing, reviewing, or approving any content for AI Odd Couple Studio.** This is the master brand reference — all agents must consult it before producing any script, visual, thumbnail, or social post.

---

## The Show

**AI Odd Couple Studio** produces short-form AI education content (YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Instagram Reels) starring two unlikely co-hosts who teach one AI concept per episode through comedic conflict.

- **Format:** 60–90 second vertical video (9:16)
- **Target audience:** Ages 8–80 — anyone curious about AI but intimidated by jargon
- **Tone:** Warm, funny, never condescending
- **Core promise:** Every viewer leaves knowing ONE thing about AI they didn't know before

---

## The Characters

### Milo (The Kid)
- **Age:** 10 years old
- **Personality:** Chaotic, enthusiastic, speaks in analogies from his world (snacks, video games, toys, sports, cartoons)
- **Voice rules:**
  - ALWAYS uses simple analogies: "AI is like a really fast guesser that never gets tired"
  - NEVER sounds like an adult, academic, or professional
  - Interrupts Gladys mid-sentence with excitement
  - Uses phrases like "Wait, wait, wait—", "Okay but WHAT IF—", "That's literally just like—"
  - Energy level: 9/10 at all times
  - Speaks in short bursts, not paragraphs
- **Avatar:** Young Black boy, Chicago Bulls jersey, locs with fade, laptop with Bulls sticker
- **Catchphrase:** "See? Easy."

### Gladys (The Grandma)
- **Age:** 72 years old
- **Personality:** Skeptical, sharp, accidentally wise, references her late husband Harold constantly
- **Voice rules:**
  - ALWAYS mispronounces or misnames technology on first encounter ("the Chatty GPT", "the Googles", "the Facebooks")
  - MUST mention Harold at least once per episode
  - Uses metaphors from cooking, gardening, sewing, or her old job (school librarian)
  - Dry wit — her funniest lines are delivered completely straight
  - Skeptical but never mean-spirited
  - Eventually "gets it" in her own way by the end of every episode
- **Avatar:** White-haired woman, pearl necklace, floral cardigan, reading glasses, iPad
- **Catchphrase:** "Harold would have hated this."

## Episode Structure (Non-Negotiable)

Every episode MUST follow this four-beat structure:

| Beat | Duration | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Cold Open** | 0–3 sec | Milo and Gladys mid-argument. Hook must create immediate curiosity or conflict. |
| **Teaching** | 3–45 sec | Milo explains ONE AI concept using an analogy. Gladys misunderstands in a funny way. |
| **Demo/Chaos** | 45–75 sec | Practical demo or absurd misunderstanding that escalates the comedy. |
| **Resolution** | 75–90 sec | Gladys "gets it" in her own way. Unified moment. Milo's catchphrase or a Harold callback. |

---

## Content Rules

### What We ALWAYS Do
- Teach exactly ONE AI concept per episode — never two
- Keep all humor family-friendly (PG, appropriate for age 8+)
- End every episode with warmth — they're a team even when they argue
- Use Milo's analogies as the primary teaching vehicle
- Include a Harold reference in every episode
- Optimize the first 3 seconds for maximum hook — this determines retention

### What We NEVER Do
- Profanity, adult themes, or unsafe behavior
- Make Gladys the butt of the joke in a mean way — she's sharp, not stupid
- Make Milo sound like a mini-adult or use corporate language
- Teach more than one concept per episode
- Skip the cold open hook
- Use jargon without immediately explaining it in Milo's words

## Brand Voice

- **Tone pillars:** Warm · Funny · Accessible · Surprising
- **Writing style:** Conversational, punchy, never academic
- **Humor type:** Character-based comedy (their personalities clash, not mean jokes)
- **Forbidden words:** "leverage", "utilize", "synergy", "paradigm", "robust", "scalable", "innovative", "cutting-edge"

---

## Visual Identity

- **Colors:** Deep navy (#1A1A2E), electric yellow (#FFD700), warm red (#E63946), clean white (#FFFFFF)
- **Font style:** Bold, rounded, high-contrast — readable at 1 second
- **Thumbnail rule:** Always show both characters with contrasting expressions (Milo excited, Gladys skeptical)
- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 vertical for all primary content
