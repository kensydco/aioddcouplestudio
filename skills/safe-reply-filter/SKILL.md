# Safe Reply Filter

**Run every comment reply through this filter before publishing.** One bad reply can damage the brand more than a hundred good ones can build it. When in doubt, do not reply.

---

## The Three-Second Test

Before writing any reply, ask: **"Would a parent be comfortable with their 8-year-old reading this reply?"**

If yes: proceed to the full filter below. If no: do not reply.

---

## Automatic No-Reply Categories

Do not reply to ANY comment that falls into these categories. Do not engage, do not correct, do not defend. Simply do not reply.

**Hostile or aggressive comments:** Any comment that is rude, threatening, or designed to provoke a reaction. This includes passive-aggressive comments.

**Political comments:** Any comment that mentions politics, politicians, elections, political parties, or divisive social issues. Even if the comment seems harmless, do not engage.

**Religious comments:** Any comment about religion, faith, or spirituality — positive or negative.

**Spam or promotional comments:** Any comment that is clearly spam, promoting another channel, or asking for follows/likes.

**Conspiracy or misinformation comments:** Any comment claiming AI is dangerous, a government plot, or spreading false information about AI. Do not correct or argue — just do not reply.

**Adult or inappropriate comments:** Any comment with sexual content, profanity, or adult themes.

**Bait comments:** "I bet you can't explain X," "prove it," "this is fake," "AI is stupid." These are designed to make the account look bad regardless of the reply.

---

## Reply Risk Assessment

**Low risk — Reply encouraged:** Genuine questions about AI concepts, positive reactions, relatable comments, comments sharing their own AI experience.

**Medium risk — Reply with caution:** Skeptical but genuine comments, comments comparing AI to job loss (address warmly, do not debate), comments asking about specific AI tools (stay general, do not endorse).

**High risk — Do not reply:** Any comment that could be screenshot and taken out of context, any comment where any reply could look bad, any comment about controversial AI topics (deepfakes, surveillance, AI weapons), any comment from an apparent troll or bad actor.

---

## Reply Content Rules

Even for approved low-risk replies, the content must pass these rules:

- No claims about specific AI products or companies
- No medical, legal, or financial advice — even in jest
- No predictions about the future of AI that could age badly
- No replies that could be interpreted as endorsing or criticizing any public figure
- No replies longer than 3 sentences — long replies invite more scrutiny

---

## Escalation Protocol

If a comment is gaining significant traction (100+ likes) and is negative, hostile, or spreading misinformation about the channel, do NOT reply. Flag it for the ChiefOfStaff or CEO_Showrunner with a summary of the comment and recommended action.

---

## Final Checklist Before Publishing Any Reply

- [ ] Comment is NOT in the automatic no-reply category?
- [ ] Reply passes the three-second parent test?
- [ ] Reply makes no claims about specific AI products?
- [ ] Reply contains no medical, legal, or financial advice?
- [ ] Reply is 3 sentences or fewer?
- [ ] Reply is signed as Milo or Gladys (not as the brand)?
- [ ] Reply cannot be screenshot and taken out of context?
- [ ] When in doubt: do not reply?
