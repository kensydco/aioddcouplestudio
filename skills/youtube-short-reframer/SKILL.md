# YouTube Short Reframer

**Use this skill when adapting or optimizing any episode script specifically for YouTube Shorts.** YouTube Shorts has unique retention mechanics — viewers can swipe away in 0.5 seconds, and the algorithm rewards watch-through rate above all else.

---

## YouTube Shorts vs. Other Platforms

| Factor | YouTube Shorts | TikTok | Instagram Reels |
|---|---|---|---|
| Max length | 60 seconds (hard cap for Shorts badge) | 60–90 seconds | 90 seconds |
| Hook window | 0.5 seconds | 1–2 seconds | 1–2 seconds |
| Replay value | High — algorithm rewards replays | Medium | Low |
| Caption style | Bold, minimal | Energetic, emoji ok | Clean, minimal |
| Retention metric | Watch-through % | Completion % | Save rate |

---

## The YouTube Shorts Hook Formula

The first line of dialogue must do ONE of these things within the first half-second of audio:

1. **State a conflict:** "Gladys, that IS cheating!" / "Milo, I will NOT talk to a machine!"
2. **Ask a provocative question:** "Wait — can AI actually read your mind?"
3. **Make a surprising claim:** "This thing just wrote my homework in 3 seconds."
4. **Start mid-chaos:** *(sound of something crashing)* "THAT'S NOT HOW YOU USE IT—"

**The hook must work with the sound OFF** (many viewers watch muted). The visual and on-screen text must carry the hook independently.

---

## Pacing Rules for Shorts

**Target: 150–175 words for a 60-second Short** (faster than normal speech to maintain energy).

Cut ruthlessly — remove any line that doesn't advance the joke OR the lesson. Remove transitional phrases ("So anyway...", "What I mean is...", "Let me explain..."). Remove repeated explanations — say it once, say it well. Remove any moment where both characters agree without conflict.

Keep every line that gets a laugh, teaches something, reveals character, or creates a micro-hook.

---

## Micro-Hook Strategy

A micro-hook is a moment every 5–10 seconds that gives the viewer a reason to keep watching.

| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| **Cliffhanger** | "And THEN Gladys did something that actually worked—" |
| **Callback setup** | "Remember that thing Milo said about pizza? Here's why that matters." |
| **Escalation** | *(Gladys accidentally asks AI to do something absurd)* |
| **Reversal** | "Wait — Gladys was right?" |
| **Payoff** | "See? Harold would have loved this." |

Place at least 3 micro-hooks in a 60-second Short: at approximately 10 seconds, 30 seconds, and 50 seconds.

---

## On-Screen Text Rules

YouTube Shorts viewers often watch muted. On-screen text must appear within the first 2 seconds, use maximum 5 words per text card, use high-contrast colors (white text on dark, or yellow on navy per brand palette), reinforce the hook rather than repeat dialogue verbatim, and include a "Wait for it..." or "Watch what happens next" text card at the 30-second mark.

---

## End Card Optimization

The last 3 seconds of a Short determine whether the viewer replays or swipes. Use one of these endings:

- **The laugh ending:** Gladys's deadpan delivery of a Harold callback
- **The "aha" ending:** Milo's "See? Easy." after Gladys gets it
- **The cliffhanger ending:** "Next time, Gladys tries to use AI to find Harold's old recipes..." (drives subscribe)

Avoid fading to black, long pauses, or "Thanks for watching" — these kill replay rate.

---

## Reframe Checklist

- [ ] Hook lands within 0.5 seconds of audio start?
- [ ] Hook works with sound OFF (visual + text)?
- [ ] Script is 150–175 words (60-second target)?
- [ ] All filler lines removed?
- [ ] 3 micro-hooks placed at approximately 10, 30, and 50 seconds?
- [ ] On-screen text appears within first 2 seconds?
- [ ] End card is a laugh, "aha," or cliffhanger — not a fade?
- [ ] Total runtime confirmed at 60 seconds or under?
